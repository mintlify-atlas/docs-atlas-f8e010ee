Flashcards in StudyQuest use a smart spaced repetition system to help you retain information effectively. Each flashcard lesson contains key concepts extracted from your study materials.

## Starting a Flashcard Session

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter Your World">
    From the Mis Mundos screen, tap on any world card to open its level map.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a Flashcard Level">
    Flashcard levels are marked with a card icon (📇). Tap on any unlocked flashcard level to begin.
    
    <Note>
      Locked levels appear gray with a lock icon. Complete previous levels to unlock them.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Start Learning">
    The flashcard session begins immediately, showing you the first card with a question on the front.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How Flashcards Work

### Card Structure

Each flashcard has two sides:

- **Front (Question)**: A concept, term, or question to recall
- **Back (Answer)**: The detailed explanation or answer

### Reviewing Cards

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the Question">
    The card displays with the question side visible. Take a moment to think about the answer.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Flip the Card">
    Tap anywhere on the card to flip it and reveal the answer. The card will smoothly rotate with a 3D animation.
    
    <Tip>
      When you flip to the answer side, StudyQuest automatically reads it aloud using text-to-speech! This helps reinforce learning through multiple senses.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assess Your Knowledge">
    After reviewing the answer, two buttons appear at the bottom:
    
    - **Red X button (Repasar)**: You didn't know this one well - needs more review
    - **Green checkmark (Lo sé)**: You got it right - you know this concept
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make Your Choice">
    Choose honestly based on whether you truly understood the concept.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Spaced Repetition System

StudyQuest uses an intelligent algorithm similar to the Anki method:

<Accordion title="Cards You Know (Green Checkmark)">
  When you mark a card as "Lo sé", it's removed from the current session. You've mastered this concept for now!
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cards to Review (Red X)">
  When you mark a card as "Repasar", it goes to the back of the queue. You'll see it again later in the same session, giving you another chance to learn it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Why This Works">
  Spaced repetition is scientifically proven to improve long-term memory retention. By reviewing difficult cards multiple times in one session, you strengthen neural pathways.
</Accordion>

## Tracking Your Progress

During your flashcard session, you'll see:

- **Progress Bar**: Visual indicator at the top showing your advancement
- **Remaining Cards Counter**: Displays "Restantes: X" to show how many cards are left
- **Completion Percentage**: Calculated based on cards you've marked as learned

<Note>
  The session only ends when all cards are marked as "Lo sé". Cards marked for review will keep appearing until you know them!
</Note>

## Audio Features

### Text-to-Speech

StudyQuest automatically reads the answer side of each flashcard:

- **Language**: Spanish (Mexico) by default
- **Speed**: Optimized for clear comprehension
- **Auto-play**: Begins immediately when you flip the card

<Tip>
  To stop the audio, simply flip the card back to the question side or move to the next card.
</Tip>

## Completing a Flashcard Level

When you've reviewed all cards:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Success Dialog Appears">
    A congratulatory message shows "¡Nivel Completado! 🎉"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Earn XP Reward">
    You receive +10 XP for completing the flashcard session. This adds to your total XP displayed in the top bar.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Unlock Next Level">
    The level is marked as complete, and the next level in your world's path unlocks automatically.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Return to Map">
    Tap "Volver al Mapa" to return to the level map and continue your learning journey.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Study Tips

<Accordion title="Be Honest with Yourself">
  Only mark cards as "Lo sé" if you truly understand the concept. It's better to review something extra than to skip content you haven't mastered.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Study in Short Sessions">
  Flashcards work best in focused 10-15 minute sessions rather than long marathon reviews. Your brain retains more with distributed practice.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Use the Audio Feature">
  Listen to the text-to-speech even if you can read the answer. Hearing information engages different memory pathways.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Review Regularly">
  Return to completed flashcard levels periodically to reinforce your long-term memory, even after you've "mastered" them.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Flip Before Judging">
  Always flip the card to check the answer before marking it, even if you think you know it. This prevents overconfidence.
</Accordion>

## Understanding Card Types

The AI generates different types of flashcards from your study materials:

- **Definition Cards**: Term on front, definition on back
- **Concept Cards**: Question about a concept, detailed explanation on back
- **Key Facts**: Important information presented as Q&A pairs
- **Process Cards**: Steps or sequences broken into memorable chunks
